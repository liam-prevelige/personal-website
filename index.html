Curious... and a Bit Chaotic

I grew up in the small farming town of Easton, CT with three older siblings. At an early age, I loved taking things apart and exploring their contents... much more than putting them back together; my room was filled with an assortment of items I discovered and promptly disassembled. Over time, this curiosity developed into an interest in building and, more generally, engineering.

In high school, I led teams working on robotics, aircraft designs for Sikorsky, and racing model cars powered by fuel cells. In addition, much of my time went toward snowboarding, soccer, tennis, lacrosse, and table tennis. In addition, I enjoyed providing goods & services to my community; I started businesses in 3D printing, tutoring, animal care, & clothing resale, and, after several serious drunk driving accidents, took on a new SafeRides program which brought in hundreds of members from the community.

Toward the end of high school, I had my first formal introduction to computer science via a new class, AP Comp Sci A. Before this, my experiences with software were limited to 'inspect element' and writing simple bash scripts. I was fascinated by how powerful computers could be and how quickly I could build digital projects relative to physical ones. After graduating valedictorian from Joel Barlow HS, I continued my education at Dartmouth College to further explore computer science and other interests.

Tech, Entrepreneurship, & Cool People

Following high school, I decided to continue exploring my interests in the intersection of software and business with a double major in computer science and economics at Dartmouth College. Now, in December of 2021, I'm a junior at Dartmouth graduating in 2023. I have completed my graduation requirements for both majors by overloading on courses, with the exception of the senior culminating experiences, but continue to explore these studies by taking additional courses I find interesting.

Early on in my Dartmouth career, I got involved in a number of clubs and organizations that helped to formalize knowledge in areas I was passionate about while growing up. Now, I serve as the chair of the student leadership board for the Magnuson Center for Entrepreneurship - Dartmouth's hub of entrepreneurial programming. I also conduct research on multirobot systems, provide investment advice for the world's largest startup accelerator, complete freelance work through a small business I made with friends, and work with a number of other clubs, organizations, and personal projects on the side. To see more detailed information about my work, check out the 'Projects & Experiences' section below!

I'm very thankful for opportunities I've received to meet incredible, passionate people in software development and other industries - including the president of Y Combinator, the CEO of Nike, leaders in startups and big tech alike, and my talented peers. My excitement for software development has grown exponentially over time. I hope to expand on this passion in the future by working for a company that cares about its customers, technology, and creates a challenging, collaborative, and rewarding environment for its employees.

Goals & Promises

Over time, it has become clear to me that any specific predictions for the future will probably be wrong. So instead, I'll focus more generally on what I want in the years ahead.

For starters, I want to be a software developer so I can continue creating and exploring my interests in the digital world. I want to work for a company where I can impact the lives of many people - whether that be fellow employees or the end consumer. I want to be in an environment where the people around me are passionate about their work and drive me to be a better version of myself. I want to be rewarded for hard work and quality output. Finally, I want to be somewhere that makes me curious, feel challenged, and allows me to explore my interests both inside and outside of work.

Of course, this is a lot to ask for and there are promises I should keep on my end. I'd like my future self to prioritize being adaptable, perseverant, focused, and a team player. I enjoy being a leader and I work well under pressure, so I promise to step up and take a leadership role at the right opportunities. Finally, I promise to try and bring a new perspective to familiar environments, while looking for ways to better myself inside and outside of work.
